I worked in collaboration with project managers and engineers to convert user's requirements into a functional design. The process involved many iteration and communication between different stakeholders.

WHAT IS A POINT-OF-SALE (POS) SYSTEM?

An explanation from Softwareadvice is a good description for the system

"A point of sale system, or POS, is the place where your customer makes a payment for products or services at your store. Simply put, every time a customer makes a purchase at your store, they’re completing a point of sale transaction.
The POS serves as the central component for your business; it’s the hub where everything—like sales, inventory and customer management—merges."

Responsibilities

Familiarize with the system
I joined the company right after my graduation. Octopus solutions is a cloud-based system which offers iPad App as POS System. The target users of the app are medium to large scale business that strives for creating innovative shopping experience and optimal management process. My first jobs was to understand the operation flow as well as the capabilities of the current system.

User Research

Re-design the app and the website
I was recruited as the first in-house designer, so my task was to take over the design from the previous outsource designer and came up with consistent branding as well as design guidelines.
After that, I had to apply the new branding on redesigning the product as well as the website.

Approach for re-design process

Established Work Flow
I was also in charge of designing new features requested by the users. I started with analyzing user pain-point and current operations, then come up with a few solutions and evaluate each solution to select the best one that can solve the user's problem.

Consolidate customer feedback
When getting feedback from the user, I had to gather and classify all of the feedback and deciding which action to take to improve the user experience.

Challenges

It was an iteration process that we had to continue working on the features every day to keep it up with the market's demand. As the primary designer of the company, I had encountered many difficulties.

  • The pressure to deliver the design on time
  • Making design decision
  • Communication problems

I've been trying my best to overcome these adversities by

  • Adopting new design tools
  • Continuous learning through the online course, meetup, and workshop
  • Boost communication within the company

Results

The new design was more aesthetic as well as usable. It had received a lot of positive feedback from the customers and played a significant role in increasing user engagement.
At the moment the new interface launched in January 2016, iTunes report showed app store view increased by 20%. Since then thousands of terminals have been deployed.
