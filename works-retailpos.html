WHAT IS A POINT-OF-SALE (POS) SYSTEM?

An explanation from Softwareadvice is a good description for the system

"A point of sale system, or POS, is the place where your customer makes a payment for products or services at your store. Simply put, every time a customer makes a purchase at your store, they’re completing a point of sale transaction.
The POS serves as the central component for your business; it’s the hub where everything—like sales, inventory and customer management—merges."

Background

I worked at Octopus Retail Management from 2015 to 2018. The POS system is the company's core product, and my primary responsibility as a full-time product designer was to own this product from concept to implemented design. The process involved many iterations and communication between different stakeholders.

It is a cloud system, and it offers an iPad app or desktop app that runs on macOS.

User personas

The primary customer of the company is mainly retail fashion stores. Using an iPad for the POS system provides a better image of the checkout counter and gives more space on the table.

The primary user of the app is the cashier or sales assistant at the store. The store manager is the casual user as they can also replace the cashier role when in need. In many cases, they need to approve some system activities, for example, if the cashier wants to void a transaction or process a refund.

Brand and color theme

I hypothesized that users might lose concentration over time by looking at monotone screens. So, I picked a colorful and contrast palette to help them quickly locate different functions and reduce user error.

Solution highlights

After investigating how cashiers usually do sales transactions, I proposed the design for the sales screen.

The sales transaction flow is breakdown into two screens. The cashier adds items, customers, salespeople, and discounts on the first screen. Then, they go to the second screen to choose payment methods and confirm the transaction. What is unique about the solution is that it allows the customer to pay in different payment modes for one transaction, for example, cash and credit card. This approach is significantly helpful for retails operated at the airport.

For the first sales screen, I proposed to have a sidebar for the customer and products selection. Since most merchants who use our POS solution have a loyalty program for their customers, they want to select the member when making the sale transaction. Therefore, having a separate sidebar allows the cashier to find the member and check the membership status faster.

Wireframe: sales screen

The cashier can quickly select the payment mode on the second screen, such as cash, visa, master, or WeChat. If a customer has been chosen, they can also pay with points earned via the loyalty program. There are three main sections: the first column is reserved for payment types, the middle has a number pad for entering the amount in cash, and the last summarise the payment details.

Results

The solution received a lot of positive feedback from the customers and played a significant role in increasing user engagement. The new interface launched in January 2016; at that time, the iTunes report showed app store view increased by 20%. Since then, thousands of terminals have been deployed.

Challenges

It was an iteration process that we had to continue working on the features every day to keep it up with the market's demand. As the primary designer of the company, I had encountered many difficulties.

  • The pressure to deliver the design on time
  • Testing and making a design decision
  • Communication problems

I've been trying my best to overcome these adversities by

  • Adopting new design tools
  • Continuous learning through the online course, meetup, and workshop
  • Boost communication within the company
